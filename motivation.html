FIND MOTIVATION

As time passes, it is possible to no longer have the same energy, the same desire to get to work, to do the work we are paid to do, productivity sometimes fails.

The loss of motivation is a delicate problem both for the employee, but also for the organization in which he operates. In this sense, developing motivation is an integral part of human resource management, and is one of the central tasks of executives and managers. To overcome this problem it would be necessary to know the sources of demotivation in the employee and then how to solve this problem.

The causes of demotivation at work

  1. 1. The feeling of belonging

    The image that the employee has of the organization has an influence enormously on his degree of involvement, commitment and especially on his motivation in the organization. In an employee with a poor representation of the organization will necessarily provide less effort in carrying out daily tasks. For the organization, managing to create a pride of belonging to the organization would be the first thing to do in the search for staff motivation.

  2. 2. The lack of recognition

    In the organization, recognition is not only linked to work results. The staff member in the performance of his duties expects premium on board to an existential recognition which recognizes him as a member of the organization having a unique expertise. Next, recognition linked to work practice, which amounts to highlighting the practices and professional skills of staff members. Finally, the recognition linked to the investment is about recognizing and thanking the efforts of each staff member.

  3. 3. Lack of promotion:

    For an employee to know that he has the possibility of evolving and climbing levels in the organization in which he evolves remains, among other things, one of the sources of motivation. AT on the other hand, working in an environment without the possibility of evolution is to find oneself in a organization or the employees make no effort in accomplishing their task but also for the collective purpose.

    The causes of demotivation are both related to the functioning of the organization in general, but also, could be related to a personal character of the member of the organization. Return his work “meaningful” for the individual who performs it constitutes a powerful source of motivation, but there is has many ways to do this, ways whose effectiveness depends as much on individuals as on the content and working environment.

Finding or rediscovering motivation

    1. Acquire new skills

    Functions within the organization evolve, the more time happens, professions evolve, forcing staff to train and be able to do their job with enthusiasm. Think about when you last learned new skills related or unrelated to your job. times.

  1. 2. Build your career plan

    To give oneself the desire to work to surpass oneself necessarily passes to define a career plan that allows you to boost your skills. The career plan should be a professional objective for any member of staff it would always be good to know their objectives short and long term to better orient themselves professionally. It would be nice to discuss it with his superior so that together we can find the best way to get there.

    Motivation remains a daily task and requires very good organization and good attitude. Defining a goal, as difficult as it is, should allow us to raise our level of competence and motivate us.
